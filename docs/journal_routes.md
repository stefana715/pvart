# Journal Routes

## Route A — SCI review / framework paper

### Best fit when
- current evidence is mainly literature + cases
- strong empirical dataset is not yet available
- the contribution is conceptual, synthetic, and framework-oriented

### Possible journal directions
- Building and Environment
- Energy and Buildings
- Journal of Building Engineering
- Renewable and Sustainable Energy Reviews
- Sustainability

### Strong angle
The strongest SCI angle is likely:
- not pure art criticism,
- not pure material engineering,
- but a structured review of how photovoltaics are integrated into architecture and public expression beyond conventional performance criteria.

### What must be present
- systematic search logic
- review matrix
- case classification
- original assessment dimensions
- critical discussion of trade-offs

---

## Route B — SCI empirical upgrade

### Best fit when
additional work can be added, such as:
- expert scoring
- questionnaire / perception study
- LCA / cost-performance comparison
- simulation of coloured versus standard PV trade-offs
- comparison between artistic / architectural / conventional deployment modes

### Benefit
This route improves novelty and increases the chance of a stronger SCI placement.

### Risk
It requires much more work and clearly defined data.

---

## Route C — Chinese core-journal paper

### Best fit when
- current material remains mainly case-based
- the contribution is design theory + classification + strategy
- the project needs to become publishable faster

### Possible Chinese direction
A paper on photovoltaic artistic expression, architectural integration logic, and public-space design strategy.

### Suitable features
- theory + cases
- architectural language
- visual and typological analysis
- practical implications for design and planning

---

## Current recommendation
At this stage, Route A is the best starting point.
Build the repository and materials to SCI standard first.
If the eventual evidence depth is insufficient, convert the project into a Chinese core paper without wasting the earlier work.
