# Review Outline

## Tentative title
From Building-Integrated Photovoltaics to Public Solar Art: A review and multidimensional assessment framework for energy-aesthetic integration

## 1. Introduction
- background: carbon neutrality and PV diffusion
- limits of purely technical adoption logic
- growing importance of aesthetics, visibility, and public acceptance
- emergence of coloured PV, media facades, solar art, and architectural PV
- research gap
- aim and contribution of this paper

- photovoltaics versus BAPV / BIPV
- coloured PV and aesthetic photovoltaics
- PV-art / solar art / public solar installations
- architectural integration versus artistic autonomy

## 3. Literature review method
- search database
- keywords
- inclusion / exclusion logic
- screening and coding process

- roof / facade / canopy / pavilion / landscape / urban furniture / public artwork
- permanent versus temporary installation
- building-attached versus free-standing
- symbolic / representational / interactive / atmospheric modes

## 5. Design strategies in existing cases
- colour and visual modulation
- transparency / translucency
- geometric composition
- patterning and image-making
- light-shadow interaction
- public engagement and interactivity
- narrative / memorial / identity-making functions

## 6. Trade-offs and constraints
- efficiency versus aesthetics
- energy visibility versus concealment
- cost and fabrication complexity
- durability / maintenance / replacement
- urban regulation and stakeholder acceptance

## 7. Proposed multidimensional assessment framework
Possible dimensions:
- technical function
- architectural integration quality
- aesthetic expression
- public interaction
- place identity contribution
- feasibility / maintainability
- replicability / scalability

## 8. Discussion
- what current PV literature misses
- what designers and planners need
- how PV-art can support broader acceptance of energy transition
- research limitations and future directions

## 9. Conclusion
- summarise the transition from PV as device to PV as cultural / spatial medium
- restate framework contribution
